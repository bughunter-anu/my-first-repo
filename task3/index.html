Naruto

Naruto is a Japanese manga series written and illustrated by Masashi Kishimoto. It tells the story of Naruto Uzumaki, a young ninja who seeks recognition from his peers and dreams of becoming the Hokage, the leader of his village. The story is told in two parts: the first is set in Naruto's pre-teen years (volumes 1–27), and the second in his teens (volumes 28–72). The series is based on two one-shot manga by Kishimoto: Karakuri (1995), which earned Kishimoto an honorable mention in Shueisha's monthly Hop Step Award the following year, and Naruto (1997).

DON'T LOVE YOU ANYMORE

A poetic guide for moving on from heartbreak and unrequited love. Author Rithvik Singh offers a comforting space to process pain and rediscover your own worth. This book is for those who feel deeply and have a hard time letting go. It's a journey toward healing and finding strength in vulnerability. The ultimate message is about loving yourself enough to leave a relationship that no longer serves you.

Nature

Nature encompasses all physical surroundings and phenomena not created by humans, including living organisms like plants and animals, and non-living elements such as weather, mountains, rivers, and the atmosphere. It represents the Earth's beauty and power, a dynamic force that sustains life, provides essential resources like clean air and water, and maintains ecological balance. Experiencing nature often brings feelings of peace and inspiration, and it is crucial to protect and preserve it for future generations.
